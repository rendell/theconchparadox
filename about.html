About The Conch Paradox

The Book

The Conch Paradox emerged from the intersection of personal crisis and professional insight. This book weaves together personal narrative, economic analysis, and natural philosophy to explore a fundamental question: Why do our greatest strengths often become our most profound vulnerabilities?

Through the lens of a family medical crisis and decades of research on Small Island Developing States, this book reveals how the protective adaptations we build—whether biological shells, specialized economies, or personal boundaries—can trap us when circumstances change dramatically.

The Author

Rendell de Kort is a development economist and PhD researcher studying economic resilience in Small Island Developing States (SIDS), with specialized focus on the Dutch Caribbean.

Professional Background:

  • Partner at Cornerstone Economics
  • World Bank Consultant (Disaster Risk Financing)
  • University of Aruba (Dutch Caribbean Digital Competence Network)
  • PhD Candidate, Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam

Rendell's work applies Complex Adaptive Systems (CAS) and Dynamic Capabilities Theory (DCT) frameworks to understand efficiency-resilience paradoxes in small island economies. His research bridges academic rigor with practical policy applications, informing disaster risk financing strategies and sustainable development initiatives across the Caribbean.
