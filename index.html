The Conch Paradox

Beyond Resilience: From Protection to Adaptation

By Rendell de Kort
Development Economist, Partner at Cornerstone Economics, and PhD researcher studying economic resilience in Small Island Developing States

"Sometimes our greatest strengths—the specialized adaptations we develop—become our most profound vulnerabilities when circumstances change dramatically."

A Tale of Two Parallel Journeys

The Personal Journey

When our unborn son was diagnosed with a severe diaphragmatic hernia, my family was thrust from the tropical certainty of Aruba into the medical complexity of Rotterdam. This journey through impossible decisions revealed profound truths about resilience, vulnerability, and the nature of strength itself.

The Professional Journey

As an economist studying Small Island Developing States, I analyze how isolated nations navigate existential threats. Geographic isolation, limited resources, climate vulnerability—these challenges demand unique adaptations that reveal universal principles about survival and transformation.

The Conch Paradox

The queen conch, native to Caribbean waters, connects these journeys. Its remarkable shell—evolved over millions of years for perfect protection—now threatens the species survival in our rapidly changing world. This paradox illuminates both personal crisis and economic theory.
