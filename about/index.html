About The Conch Paradox

A Journey Through Crisis and Discovery

When our unborn son was diagnosed with a severe diaphragmatic hernia, my family found ourselves thrust into a world of impossible medical decisions. Our journey from Aruba to Rotterdam revealed profound insights about the nature of resilience—both personal and systemic.

The Conch Metaphor

The queen conch, a marine mollusk native to Caribbean waters, embodies the paradox of resilience. Its magnificent shell, evolved over millions of years for protection, now makes it vulnerable to climate change and overfishing. This same pattern—where our greatest strengths become potential weaknesses—appears everywhere from island economies to human health.

Who This Book Is For

  • Readers interested in economic resilience and island studies
  • Parents navigating medical challenges
  • Anyone facing personal or professional transformation
  • Policy makers working on climate adaptation
  • Those curious about how nature’s wisdom applies to modern challenges

About the Author

Rendell de Kort is a PhD researcher in economics focusing on economic resilience in Small Island Developing States (SIDS). As founder of Cornerstone Economics and consultant for the World Bank, he bridges academic research with practical application.
