About The Conch Paradox

The Book

The Conch Paradox emerged from the intersection of personal crisis and professional insight. When our family faced a life-threatening medical emergency with our unborn son, the journey from Aruba to Rotterdam became more than a geographic transitionâ€”it became a profound exploration of resilience itself.

This book weaves together:

  • Personal Narrative: Our family’s journey through impossible medical decisions
  • Economic Analysis: How Small Island Developing States navigate existential threats
  • Natural Wisdom: The queen conch as a perfect metaphor for resilience paradoxes

The Author

Rendell de Kort

Rendell de Kort is a PhD researcher in economics focusing on economic resilience and adaptive capacity in Small Island Developing States (SIDS), with specific focus on the Dutch Caribbean.

<p>Born and raised in Aruba, educated in the Netherlands, Rendell brings a unique bicultural perspective to questions of island resilience. His work bridges the gap between rigorous academic research and practical policy application.</p>

<p>The personal medical journey detailed in <em>The Conch Paradox</em> transformed his understanding of resilience from an abstract economic concept to a lived reality, enriching his professional work with deep personal insight.</p>

<p>Rendell lives in Aruba with his wife and three children, where he continues his research while maintaining his commitment to making economic insights accessible to underserved communities.</p>
