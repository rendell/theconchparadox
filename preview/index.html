Preface

This book was born on the Erasmus bridge in Rotterdam. As an economist studying resilience in Small Island Developing States, I’ve spent years analyzing vulnerability indices, economic diversification strategies, and adaptive governance frameworks. My professional work has focused on understanding how island nations like my home of Aruba navigate their unique challenges: geographic isolation, limited resources, exposure to climate risks, and economic dependencies.

Yet it wasn’t until my family faced our own resilience test that I truly began to understand the human dimensions of these abstract concepts. Our journey through medical crisis, displacement, and eventual recovery provided a deeply personal lens through which to view the broader questions of resilience that have defined my academic career.
